How It Works

Three things Plotra does

Structures land first

Every farm is mapped as a parcel. All records — deliveries, documents, compliance data — are anchored to that parcel identity. This is what makes traceability verifiable.

Verifies in layers

Field-captured data passes through cooperative review, geospatial analysis, and system-level quality checks. Each layer builds on the last.

Corrects rather than rejects

When gaps arise, Plotra opens a structured rectification process. Records are corrected, re-linked, and completed — without discarding valid data.

For Importers

Compliance you can rely on — built for real supply chains

EUDR compliance requires more than documentation. It requires a clear, verifiable link between a commodity and the land it came from — supported by auditable records and consistent data structures.

If your suppliers have no structured data: Plotra builds it from the ground up — farmer and parcel registration, geospatial mapping, polygon validation, satellite-supported verification, cooperative validation workflows, and DDS-ready outputs.

If you already receive geolocation data: Plotra provides a structured intake and validation layer. Geodata is normalised, documents are linked, inconsistencies are resolved, and DDS-ready outputs are generated.

If you need geospatial analysis only: Plotra can assess existing polygon data — validating structure, checking deforestation-free status, and identifying boundary issues.

Why Plotra is Different
  • Parcel-first architecture — compliance is anchored in land identity
  • Rectification workflows — errors are structured and corrected, not rejected
  • Climate-aware interpretation — agroforestry and intercropping systems are interpreted over time

Data You Can Rely On

Plotra provides importers with access to structured compliance records through a dedicated interface — parcel-linked datasets, linked documentation, DDS-ready submissions, and audit logs — so records remain accessible for future use, regulatory audits, and continuity across reporting cycles.

For Cooperatives & Exporters

Making smallholder trade legible

Plotra is built for the realities of smallholder agriculture — fragmented plots, shared boundaries, customary tenure, and agroforestry systems where multiple crops grow under a single canopy. These are not exceptions. They are the norm.

When compliance systems fail to account for this complexity, valid farms appear incomplete and producers risk exclusion — not because of wrongdoing, but because their reality does not fit simplified models.

What It Means in Practice
  • Each farm is mapped as a parcel with a clear identity
  • Production data is linked to that parcel and verified through cooperative review
  • Issues are corrected through structured rectification, not rejected
  • Compliance outputs are generated in EUDR-compatible formats

Data Governance

Plotra Data Principles

Data should support participation in trade — not create dependency or extraction.

Purpose-Limited Data Use

Data is collected and used only for supply-chain functions including EUDR compliance, traceability, and value attribution. It is not used for profiling, advertising, or resale.

Farmer and Cooperative Access

Farmers and cooperatives retain access to parcel-linked data. Records can be retrieved and exported in standard formats for use across buyers and markets.

No Extractive Data Practices

Data generated within Plotra is not bundled, resold, or converted into standalone commercial datasets. It remains linked to the supply-chain context.

Interoperability

Standard data formats allow compliance datasets and parcel records to be shared across regulatory, commercial, and cooperative systems.

Transparent System Logic

Compliance workflows and validation logic are versioned and auditable. Decisions affecting compliance status remain traceable over time.

Privacy and Security

Personal data is processed in accordance with GDPR — including data minimisation, role-based access controls, and secure storage practices.

Data Sovereignty

Farmers and cooperatives retain visibility and functional access to their data. Participation in Plotra does not restrict future market relationships.

  • Your data supports your access to market
  • Your records remain usable across buyers
  • Your participation does not lock you into a single system

About Plotra

Plotra is the operational infrastructure behind Terraxis' compliance and traceability services.

It is the system through which land is mapped, data is structured, verification is applied, and compliance outputs are generated.

It is designed to absorb complexity — not eliminate it. Fragmented land, layered tenure, mixed production systems, and incomplete data are not treated as problems to be filtered out. They are structured into verifiable, auditable records.

Plotra is currently in active deployment through the Kenya pilot programme.

Built on Open Standards

Plotra is designed in alignment with Digital Public Goods principles — focused on interoperability, transparency, and avoiding black-box dependency. The infrastructure is structured to remain legible and portable across systems.
