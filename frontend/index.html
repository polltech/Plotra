Plotra

Parcel-based infrastructure for EUDR compliance and supply chain traceability

Plotra structures the data behind smallholder agriculture — linking land, production, and cooperative verification into auditable records that meet regulatory requirements.

It works with the realities of fragmented land, mixed systems, and incomplete data. It does not require perfect inputs. It structures them.

How It Works

Three things Plotra does

1

Structures land first

Every farm is mapped as a parcel. All records — deliveries, documents, compliance data — are anchored to that parcel identity. This is what makes traceability verifiable rather than claimed.

2

Verifies in layers

Field-captured data passes through cooperative review, geospatial and environmental analysis, and system-level quality checks. Each layer builds on the last.

3

Corrects rather than rejects

When gaps or inconsistencies arise, Plotra opens a structured rectification process. Records are corrected, re-linked, and completed — without discarding valid production data.

For Importers

Compliance you can rely on — built for real supply chains

EUDR compliance requires more than documentation. It requires a clear, verifiable link between a commodity and the land it came from — supported by auditable records and consistent data structures.

Plotra supports importers depending on where their supply chain data currently stands.

If your suppliers have no structured data, Plotra builds it from the ground up — farmer and parcel registration, geospatial mapping, polygon validation, satellite-supported verification, cooperative validation workflows, and DDS-ready outputs.

If you already receive geolocation data or supplier declarations, Plotra provides a structured intake and validation layer. Geodata is normalised, documents are linked, inconsistencies are resolved, and DDS-ready outputs are generated — without replacing your existing supplier relationships.

If you need geospatial analysis only, Plotra can assess existing polygon data — validating structure, checking deforestation-free status, interpreting canopy and intercropping systems, and identifying boundary issues — without requiring full system integration.

Why Plotra is Different

Most compliance systems filter data — records are accepted or rejected. That works in standardised supply chains. It breaks down in smallholder systems.

  • Parcel-first architecture — compliance is anchored in land identity, not documents or shipments
  • Rectification workflows — errors are structured, corrected, and versioned instead of triggering exclusion
  • Climate-aware interpretation — agroforestry, intercropping, and canopy systems are interpreted over time, reducing false risk signals

Operational Impact

  • Reduced compliance risk
  • Structured, audit-ready datasets
  • Less manual reconciliation of supplier inputs
  • The ability to work with real supply chains, not idealised ones

For Cooperatives & Exporters

Making smallholder trade legible

Plotra is built for the realities of smallholder agriculture — fragmented plots, shared boundaries, customary tenure, and agroforestry systems where multiple crops grow under a single canopy. These are not exceptions. They are the norm.

When compliance systems fail to account for this complexity, valid farms appear incomplete and producers risk exclusion — not because of wrongdoing, but because their reality does not fit simplified models. Plotra addresses this directly.

What It Means in Practice

  • Each farm is mapped as a parcel with a clear identity
  • Production data is linked to that parcel and verified through cooperative review
  • Issues are corrected through structured rectification, not rejected
  • Compliance outputs are generated in formats compatible with EUDR requirements

Data Governance

Plotra Data Principles

Data should support participation in trade — not create dependency or extraction.

Purpose-Limited Data Use

Data is collected and used only for supply-chain functions including EUDR compliance, traceability, and value attribution linked to production practices. It is not used for profiling, advertising, or resale.

Farmer and Cooperative Access

Farmers and cooperatives retain access to parcel-linked data associated with their participation. Records can be retrieved and exported in standard formats for use across buyers, markets, and compliance processes.

No Extractive Data Practices

Data generated within Plotra is not bundled, resold, or converted into standalone commercial datasets. It remains linked to the supply-chain context in which it was created.

Interoperability and Portability

Standard data formats and interoperable structures allow compliance datasets, parcel records, and verification outputs to be shared across regulatory, commercial, and cooperative systems.

Transparent System Logic

Compliance workflows and validation logic are versioned and auditable. Decisions affecting compliance status or economic outcomes remain traceable over time.

Privacy and Security

Personal data is processed in accordance with GDPR — including data minimisation, role-based access controls, and secure storage practices.

Data Sovereignty

Farmers and cooperatives retain visibility and functional access to their data. Participation in Plotra does not restrict future market relationships or create dependency on a single platform.

  • Your data supports your access to market
  • Your records remain usable across buyers
  • Your participation does not lock you into a single system

About Plotra

Plotra is the operational infrastructure behind Terraxis' compliance and traceability services.

It is the system through which land is mapped, data is structured, verification is applied, and compliance outputs are generated.

It is designed to absorb complexity — not eliminate it. Fragmented land, layered tenure, mixed production systems, and incomplete data are not treated as problems to be filtered out. They are structured into verifiable, auditable records that regulatory frameworks and buyers can rely on.

Plotra is currently in active deployment through the Kenya pilot programme, working with sample cooperative societies and registered farmers.

Built on Open Standards

Plotra is designed in alignment with Digital Public Goods principles — focused on interoperability, transparency of system logic, and the avoidance of black-box dependency. The infrastructure is structured to remain legible and portable across systems.

Within this structure, Terraxis develops commercial service layers that build on the underlying infrastructure — maintaining a clear distinction between shared data foundations and market-facing applications.
